## Core Problem

Kaya has **failure to thrive with severe feeding disorder** of unclear etiology. She has been nasogastric tube-fed since approximately 5 months of age. Despite extensive workup across multiple specialties, no unifying diagnosis has been found.

**Key features:**
- Cascade vomiting since 2 months of age
- Total food refusal / oral aversion (improving recently)
- Failure to thrive: weight -2.5 to -3 SD, length -2.5 SD, head circumference -2 SD
- Motor developmental delay (catching up)
- Stereotypical bilateral circular hand movements
- Recurrent infections (13+ episodes in 14 months despite isolation from daycare)
- NG tube since ~5 months; fed Althera (extensively hydrolyzed formula) 850 mL/day

## What Has Been Ruled Out

Pancreatic insufficiency, celiac disease, iron deficiency, B12 deficiency, folate deficiency, remethylation defects, liver disease, kidney disease, primary hypothyroidism, chronic inflammation, anatomical GI obstruction (normal upper GI series), adenoid hypertrophy (normal fiberscopy), and hip dysplasia.

## What Remains Unresolved

1. **No unifying diagnosis** for the symptom complex
2. **Genetics** -- referral to clinical genetics (Karolinska) sent Dec 2025, awaiting appointment. No genetic testing done.
3. **Immunology** -- IgG subclasses and lymphocyte subpopulations accepted by treating physician but not yet drawn
4. **Eosinophilic esophagitis** -- never ruled out (requires endoscopy with biopsies)
5. **Free T4** -- was at lower limit of reference (12 pmol/L, ref 12-26) in Aug 2025, never rechecked
6. **Microcytic blood picture without iron deficiency** -- likely beta-thalassemia trait (given father's Iranian background), Hb electrophoresis not yet done
7. **IGF-1 slightly below reference** (17 vs ref 18-172) -- likely nutritional, requires follow-up
