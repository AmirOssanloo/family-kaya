### Growth Pattern Summary

All three parameters (weight, length, HC) have shown proportional decline from near-normal at birth to approximately -2 to -3 SD by 12-15 months. Weight was affected first (by ~2 months), followed by length, and then head circumference -- a pattern consistent with nutritional failure to thrive. However, the lack of catch-up growth despite 7+ months of NG tube feeding (850 mL Althera/day providing ~560 kcal) is notable and raises the question of whether there is an intrinsic growth disorder in addition to the nutritional component.

## Feeding History in Detail

### Phase 1: Birth to 2 months (Breastfeeding)
- Initially breastfed, described as adequate
- Gained weight normally in the first weeks

### Phase 2: 2-5 months (Onset & Escalation)
- **~2 months:** Onset of cascade vomiting. Oral candidiasis.
- Suspected CMPA (cow's milk protein allergy). Milk-specific IgE: 0.74 (weakly positive). Mother placed on dairy-free diet while breastfeeding.
- PPI trial (Nexium/esomeprazole) -- partial response: reduced frequency of vomiting but did not eliminate it. Discontinued after ~1 month.
- Switch to Althera (extensively hydrolyzed formula) -- some initial improvement.
- Progressive refusal of bottle. Would only breastfeed for comfort.
- Weight curve declining from -1 to -2 to -3 SD.

### Phase 3: 5-12 months (NG Tube, Total Oral Aversion)
- **July 2025:** NG tube first placed during hospitalization (adenovirus + norovirus + dehydration).
- Initially intended as temporary -- became permanent as Kaya refused all oral intake.
- **Total food refusal.** Would not accept bottle, spoon, cup, or any solid food.
- Described by multiple clinicians as "afraid of food" -- flinches, blinks, closes eyes when food approaches mouth.
- Paradoxically, puts toys in mouth but not food -- suggesting **sensory/behavioral aversion** rather than oral motor problem.
- Speech therapy assessment: good oral motor function, no swallowing problems. Diagnosis: **Pediatric Feeding Disorder (PFD) with sensory hypersensitivity.**
- Tube feeding: Althera 700 mL/day, increased to 850 mL/day over time.
- Vomiting continued with tube feeds -- volume-dependent and speed-dependent. Feeds given slowly over ~1.5 hours per session.
- Breast still accepted at night for comfort.

### Phase 4: 12-15 months (Gradual Improvement -- Current)
- **Jan 2026:** Spontaneous interest in food emerges.
- Tasting porridge (oatmeal), bread, bread with butter.
- Eating 5-10 bites per meal.
- Drinking small amounts of formula from straw cup/bottle with straw (30-90 mL/day).
- Vomiting much less frequent than before.
- When tube feeding pump broke, showed hunger and drank larger volumes.
- **Current regimen:** NG tube 150 mL x 5/day (~750 mL) + 100 mL evening supplement. Still breastfed at night.
- Gastrostomy discussed at team visit (Jan 8, 2026) -- parents declined, wish to observe continued oral progress. Offered to be placed on waiting list (3-month wait).

## Nutritional Calculations

### Current Intake (estimated)
- **Tube:** ~850 mL Althera/day = ~560 kcal (Althera provides ~66 kcal/100 mL)
- **Oral:** ~60-90 mL formula + ~1 tablespoon solid food = ~50-80 kcal
- **Breastfeeding (night):** Unknown volume, estimated ~50-100 kcal
- **Total estimated:** ~660-740 kcal/day

### Estimated Caloric Need
- For a 15-month-old girl at -2.5 SD weight: basal need ~700-750 kcal/day
- For catch-up growth: 120-150% of normal = **~900-1000 kcal/day**
- **Current intake likely insufficient for catch-up growth**

### Key Nutritional Question

The treating team has discussed **caloric fortification** of tube feeds (e.g., adding rapeseed oil, MCT oil, or switching to a higher-calorie formula like Infatrini at 100 kcal/100 mL vs Althera at 66 kcal/100 mL). This has been accepted by the senior gastroenterologist and is pending dietitian implementation.
