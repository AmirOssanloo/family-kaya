## Key Clinical Questions for Consulting Physician

We would welcome your perspective on the following:

### 1. The Feeding Disorder
Kaya's vomiting and total oral aversion began at ~2 months of age, which is unusually early for a purely behavioral feeding disorder (ARFID/PFD). The sensory hypersensitivity pattern (flinches at food, accepts toys in mouth) has been documented but the underlying cause remains unclear. **Is this consistent with a purely sensory/behavioral disorder, or should an organic cause be pursued more aggressively (e.g., EoE, GI dysmotility)?**

### 2. Failure to Catch Up on Tube Feeding
Despite 7+ months of NG tube feeding (Althera ~560 kcal/day from tube, plus breastfeeding), Kaya has not achieved catch-up growth. She follows her own curve at -2.5 to -3 SD. **Is the caloric intake simply insufficient, or does the lack of catch-up suggest an intrinsic growth disorder?** The recent finding of IGF-1 below reference (17, ref 18-172) is relevant here.

### 3. Recurrent Infections
13+ infections in 14 months in a child NOT in daycare, with the older sibling kept home from daycare to reduce exposure, is notable. Baseline immunoglobulins are normal. Transient severe lymphopenia (1.9, ref 3.6-12) was seen during acute infection and normalized. **Does this warrant further immunological workup beyond IgG subclasses and lymphocyte subpopulations?**

### 4. The Microcytic Picture
Consistently low EVF (5 measurements) and now low MCV (75) without iron deficiency (ferritin 68). Father is Iranian (beta-thalassemia carrier frequency 10-15%). **Is Hb electrophoresis the correct next step, or should anything else be considered?**

### 5. Genetic Workup
The symptom complex -- FTT + feeding disorder + motor delay + stereotypies + infection susceptibility + hypertrichosis + hypermobility -- spans multiple organ systems. Three specialists (neurologist, two geneticists at conference) have not identified syndromic features clinically. The genetics conference was based on photographs, not physical examination. **In your experience, does this presentation warrant genetic testing (e.g., trio-WES/WGS) regardless of the lack of overt dysmorphic features?**

### 6. Anything Else?
Given the full clinical picture, **are there diagnoses or investigations you would consider that have not been addressed?**
