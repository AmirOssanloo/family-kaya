### MCV -- Declining (Microcytic Trend)

```
Date:       Aug-29  Feb-11
MCV:        79      75      (reference: 76-94)
```

MCV has dropped below reference and is trending downward -- unusual as MCV normally increases with age.

### Interpretation of Microcytic Picture Without Iron Deficiency

With ferritin 68 (normal) and CRP <1 (no inflammation), iron deficiency is definitively excluded. The most likely explanation is **beta-thalassemia trait**, given:
- Father's Iranian background (10-15% carrier frequency in Iran)
- Classic hematological picture: low MCV, low MCH, normal-to-high RBC count (4.6), relatively preserved Hb, normal ferritin
- **Hb electrophoresis/HPLC has not been performed** (recommended)

### IGF-1 -- New Finding

IGF-1 at 17 (ref 18-172) is just below the lower limit. In the context of chronic FTT and undernutrition, this is most likely **nutritional suppression of the GH-IGF-1 axis**. However, IGFBP-3 is normal, which is the typical pattern in nutritional FTT (IGF-1 drops before IGFBP-3). If IGF-1 remains low after 3 months of optimized nutrition, GH stimulation testing should be considered.
