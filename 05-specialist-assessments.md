## Gastroenterology / Nutrition (Sachsska Children's Hospital)

**Lead physician:** Petter Malmborg (gastroenterologist)
**Team:** Dietitian, nutrition nurse (Therese Fjallid, Frida Andersson), ST physician (Sveborg)

### Assessment Summary
- Primary working diagnosis: **Pediatric Feeding Disorder (PFD)** with secondary failure to thrive
- Suspected CMPA (cow's milk protein allergy) based on weakly positive milk-IgE (0.74) and temporal correlation. Managed with Althera (extensively hydrolyzed formula). No formal milk challenge ever performed.
- PPI trial (esomeprazole) at ~3 months for suspected GERD -- partial response on vomiting frequency, discontinued after ~1 month
- NG tube placed July 2025, remains in place
- Upper GI series (Aug 2025): normal -- no anatomical obstruction
- Fecal elastase: normal -- pancreatic insufficiency excluded
- Senior gastroenterologist (Feb 2026 meeting, 30 years experience) assessed that:
  - EoE is unlikely and gastroscopy not indicated at this time
  - Genetic testing is not their domain -- defers to genetics
  - Thyroid re-check unnecessary ("extremely rare at her age")
  - Accepted: ferritin, IgG subclasses, lymphocyte subpopulations, folate/homocysteine re-draw
- Gastrostomy discussed (Jan 2026) -- parents declined for now given positive oral feeding trend
- Caloric fortification of tube feeds recommended (pending dietitian implementation)

### Key Unanswered Questions from Gastro Perspective
1. Why is there no catch-up growth despite 7+ months of NG tube feeding (~560 kcal/day from tube alone)?
2. Should gastroscopy be performed to rule out EoE (eosinophilic esophagitis)?
3. Is the feeding disorder purely sensory/behavioral (ARFID/PFD) or is there an underlying organic cause?

## Pediatric Neurology (Sachsska Children's Hospital)

**Lead physician:** Nathalie Palmberg (pediatric neurologist)

### Assessment Summary (Two formal evaluations: Aug 25, 2025 and Nov 18, 2025)

**Motor Development:**
- Delayed but progressing
- At 9 months (Aug 2025): sitting independently, holding head up prone, but not crawling, not pulling to stand. Difficulty holding head up when placed supine. No pincer grasp attempts.
- At 12 months (Nov 2025): improved significantly. Sitting well, starting to pull to stand, pincer grasp present bilaterally.
- At 13 months (Jan 2026, physiotherapy): crawling reciprocally, pulling to stand, cruising, standing briefly without support.

**Stereotypies:**
- Bilateral circular hand/wrist movements noted since ~8 months
- Persistent, not distractible, independent of mood
- Also noted: arms extended backward/behind body at times
- Neurologist assessment: **benign motor stereotypies** -- not indicative of specific neurological condition

**Neurological Examination:**
- Good muscle tone
- Good muscle strength
- Normal reflexes
- No pathological movement patterns
- No signs of spasticity

**Conclusions (Nathalie Palmberg):**
- **No signs of cerebral palsy**
- **No signs of neuromuscular disease**
- **No syndromic features identified**
- **No signs of mitochondrial disorder** (based on clinical picture and normal lactate -- note: normal lactate does not fully exclude mitochondrial disease)
- Motor delay is real but not pathological in pattern -- likely multifactorial (undernutrition + general condition)
- Recommended continued physiotherapy follow-up
- Ordered metabolic screening (normal) and upper GI series (normal)
- Sent referral to clinical genetics in December 2025
